Assumptions and Errors

Question 1

Initially I assumed I could go as far as to find the chemical reactions that occur between cetirizine HCl and a certain amount of a specific allergen. However, when I conducted research, I went from cetirizine HCl to the antiallergenic effects of the H1 receptor antagonists. My search took me from the chemical down to the gene that controls interactions with environmental allergens. The allergens themselves did not have a basic chemical equation, because they are composed of many other factors such as dust, mites, and pollen. Along with further understanding of the project, I came to realize I need only quantify attributes of cetirizine HCl rather than its interactions with the body and allergens.


Question 2

I made errors in the presentation of information in this question. I believed a build up of the equation would serve to better inform the audience. However, scientific presentation of dimensional analysis requires that all information be shown in one line of equation. Since I was analyzing 5 elements, I thought to make equations for each of them. This was also not a good idea, since my website would then be littered with equations that were missing some units. I went with a blank equation, where I had a table of information above it so that the audience can plug in the values and then find the results. Of course, I had the results in the subsequent table. I also added a "check my answer" equation, where it shows that adding up the grams of each element in the 10 mg of cetirizine HCl adds up to 10 mg almost perfectly.

This is influenced by false information I acquired in a previous version of question 2.
