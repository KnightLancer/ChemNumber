Question 2. How many milligrams of carbon/hydrogen/chlorine/nitrogen/oxygen are in 10 mg of cetirizine HCl?

The process:

This was also a tricky question. First, I found the atomic masses of each of the elements in C21H26Cl2N2O3. These values are the atomic masses out of 1 mole of cetirizine HCl. This became the first half of a proportion conversion:

atomic mass⁄1 mol C21H26Cl2N2O3

The other half of the conversion was the missing variable "x" (the atomic masses of said elements in 10 mg) over the amount of moles in 10 mg of cetirizine HCl (0.00002351005 mol):

x⁄0.00002351005 mol

And thus the conversion:

atomic mass⁄1 mol C21H26Cl2N2O3 = x⁄0.00002351005 mol

However that was not it. The result is still in atomic mass units. In order to convert from atomic mass units (amu) to grams, you must multiple amu by 1.66 x 10-24 grams/amu.

AND THEN to get from grams to milligrams, you divide by 1000.
