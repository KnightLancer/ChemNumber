Question 1. How many moles are in 10 mg of cetirizine HCl?


The process:

As said before, I really wanted to go down to the atomic relationships of allergens, but they would not be so easy to quanitfy. I therefore thought this question was a good first one. Sourcing PubChem, a credible source and the nation's library of medicine, the molecular weight of cetirizine HCl is 425.35 grams per mole (g/mol). From there I used this conversion factor:

I took 10 mg, which was the amount of C21H26Cl2N2O3 in one tablet. Since the conversion factor had grams, I converted 10 mg to grams by dividing 10 mg by 1000, since there are 1000 mg in a gram.

The result was 0.00002351005 moles.
